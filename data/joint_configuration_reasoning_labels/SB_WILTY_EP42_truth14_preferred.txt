However, these same behaviors can also be consistent with thoughtful engagement or sincerity, especially if the person is carefully considering their words or feeling earnest.

The presence of gaze sideways combined with subtle head movements forward and backward suggests a dynamic interaction rather than avoidance. People who are lying often avoid eye contact entirely or exhibit rigid, unnatural postures. In this case, the mixed head orientations and movements may indicate openness and responsiveness rather than evasiveness. Additionally, an open arm posture generally conveys openness and a lack of defensiveness, which leans toward honesty rather than concealment.

That said, it's important to acknowledge that none of these behaviors alone or even combined can guarantee truthfulness with absolute certainty. Some individuals can exhibit nervous habits even when telling the truth, while skilled deceivers might consciously mimic honest cues. Still, considering the range and interplay of signals—the engaged gaze, open posture, and natural head movements—the behavior aligns more closely with genuine communication rather than deception. Given this, the educated assessment leans toward the person telling the truth, although it remains a cautious conclusion rather than an absolute certainty.
